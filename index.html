- Annie Leonard

Our stop-motion animation, Plastic Wave, is meant to help viewers visualize and understand the effects of water pollution on the environment and its organisms. Water pollution, of any form, negatively affects the wildlife and health of the ocean. It can never be reversed, but with the awareness and action of the people, it can be forestalled.

⸻ The Purpose ⸻

Plastic pollution is one of the world's gravest problems. Supported by corporate power abuse and the individuals’ negligence on the severity of the issue, plastics' presence in our oceans continues to grow despite the multitude of benevolent campaigns and fundraisers created to combat this issue.
To show and educate others on some of the issues that contribute to more plastic in our oceans, I created an interactive loopy model (format provided by Nicky Case).

⸻ The Timeline ⸻

Day 1: First Drafts

The first day of our animation process has begun. First, we created the initial drafts and storyboards of our separate animation ideas. My idea was originally meant to depict a piranha tank that slowly got littered over time and eventually, a hand would reach in to clean the tank. I wanted to show some distrust and wariness between the hand and piranhas, so I was thinking of having the piranhas bite the hand (this idea was scrapped because we didn’t feel the scene would properly convey the point). After all of the litter was cleaned out of the tank, the piranhas would offer their treasure to the hand, and the story would close.

Day 4: Filming!

On the fourth day, we finished more than half of our animation. With the beginning and flashback scenes done, we all were confident we would be able to finish the animation on the fifth day.

Day 5: Done, and Shipped

On the fifth day, we finished filming our animation! There was a little disagreement on how the video would end and we ended up deviating from the original storyboard, but we were able to find a way to tie the animation together. In addition, everything on DragonFrame was ready to be edited by Thomas, so the class ended perfectly on schedule with the deadline of the assignment.

Day 6: Final Touches

The sixth day was the deadline for the assignment, so there was a hectic atmosphere in class. Thomas was able to edit and add music to the video over the weekend, so feedback was the only thing we were focused on during that class period. Unfortunately, we didn't have enough time to get a peer review from another group that also was doing stop-motion animation, but that wasn't of importance to us. With the edited video done and no more feedback to give, I was able to turn in my assignment on January 15, 2023.
